Overview

Variety registration is the official process of adding a new plant variety to Egypt's National Variety Register. This process ensures that new varieties are distinct, uniform, stable, and valuable for cultivation and use.

‚ÑπÔ∏è
Important: Registration is mandatory for commercial seed production and sale in Egypt. It is separate from Plant Variety Protection (PVP), which provides intellectual property rights.

Step-by-Step Process

1
Prepare Application
Week 1
‚ñº

What you need:

  • Completed application form (Form VR-01)
  • Technical description of variety
  • Photos showing key characteristics
  • Gene bank deposit certificate
  • Payment receipt

Required Information:

Your application must include:

  • Proposed variety name (must be unique)
  • Breeder and maintainer details
  • Breeding history and pedigree
  • Preliminary DUS characteristics
  • Known performance data (if available)

What happens next:

  • Application receives unique serial number

3
DUS Testing
Months 3-20 (2 years)

Testing Requirements:

  • Two consecutive growing seasons required
  • UPOV Test Guidelines followed
  • Field location determined by CASC
  • Breeder must provide sufficient seed quantity

Your Role:

During DUS testing, you may be required to:

  • Provide additional seed if needed
  • Clarify technical questions
  • Attend field inspections (optional)

VCU Testing Requirements:

Vegetables: Exempt (except onion)

  • Decision made within 2 months
